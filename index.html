Abstract

Given an image query, your goal is to retrieve the relevant images. You may extract HoG and CNN features using the provided implementation and try different approaches, including classification and clustering-based techniques.


Motivation

The exponential growth of digital images across the internet has led to an increased demand for efficient and accurate methods for organizing, searching and retrieving images. Traditional text-based search engines often fail to adequately address the needs of users seeking visual content, as they heavily rely on textual annotations which may be subjective and incomplete. This limitation has motivated the development of content-based image retrieval systems (CBIR systems), which aim to retrieve images based on their visual features rather than textual prompts. Our project is a scale-down version of today’s state-of-the-art image retrieval systems such as ‘Google Lens’, ‘Image Search’, etc.


Introduction

Image Retrieval is a fundamental and long-standing computer vision task that involves finding images similar to a provided query from a large database. It’s often considered as a form of fine-grained, instance-level classification. Not just integral to image recognition alongside classification and detection, it also holds substantial business value by helping users to discover images aligning with their interests or requirements, guided by visual similarity or other parameters.
